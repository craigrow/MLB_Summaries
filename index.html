In a game that saw the Nationals rally from a late deficit, Cionel Pérez picked up the win as Washington edged the Cardinals 9-6 in the series opener on Friday night. The sparse crowd of 12,319 watched as the home team turned a 6-3 eighth-inning deficit into a thrilling comeback, capped by a three-run homer from CJ Abrams.

Ramón Urías provided a valiant effort for St. Louis, going 3-for-4 with a home run and three RBIs, but it wasn’t enough to fend off a surging Nationals offense. Brady House also contributed significantly for Washington, collecting three hits and driving in two runs, while James Wood's two hits—including a pivotal home run—helped fuel the late-game explosion that secured the victory and gave the Nationals a much-needed boost.
